Trandal posthuset, "Trandal krins" og Statens navnekonsulenten...

     In 197__ the Norwegian State's "Name Consultant" determined that Tranndal (with 2 NNs) should thereafter be the only "correct" spelling of Trandal's Post Office. This was based on an academic consultant's theoretical understanding of how local people pronounced the name. He then transformed the oral word over to the standard government written format. (There was no input from Trandalers themselves.)

     After local protests spearheaded by the "TRANDAL KRINS" organization hit the newspapers, the State Post Office was forced to remove the controversial artificial word "Tranndal" from postal use, including signage, maps, brochures, etc., and returned again to use the original "Trandal" as the official name spelling. It is highly unlikely that any family was ever named Tranndal, written with 2 NNs...
