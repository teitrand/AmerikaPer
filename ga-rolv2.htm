GANGER-ROLV   - - -   "Ralph Walker"

    Den islandske skald Snorre Sturlason (d.1241 i alderen 63) samlet Kongesagaer.. og der kan ein lese om Ganger-Rolv av Møre.

    The Icelandic skald (historian-poet) Snorri Sturlasson (d.1241 at age 63) collected the Sagas (histories) of the Kings.. and there one can read about "Ralph Walker" of Møre. [Sunnmøre = Southern Møre]                        

Walker


~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
Kong Harald i gjestebod hos far til Gange-Rolv

King Harold visits Gange-Rolv's father.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

      Kong Harald var i gjestebod på Møre hos Ragnvald jarl; då hadde han lagt heile landet under seg. Då gjekk kongen i bad der. Og no lot kong Harald håret sitt greie, og Ragnvald jarl skar håret hans; då hadde det ikkje vært skåret eller kjemmet på ti år. Før kalte dei han Harald Luva, men no gav Ragnvald jarl han nytt namn, og kalte han Harald Hårfagre. Alle som så han, sa at det var verkeleg eit sant namn, for han hadde eit hår som var både stort og vakkert.


      After King Harold had united Norway by force, he visited Møre as the guest of Ragnvald, Earl of Møre. ["Jarl" = Duke? Count? Scandinavian nobleman or chieftain. The Old Norse word is akin to "Earl"] Earl Ragnvald cut the king's hair and gave him a new name: Harold-the-Fair-Haired.. The king's hair had been neither cut nor combed for 10 years [True to his vow to the beautiful Gyda, daughter of King Eirik of Hordaland, he had not cut his hair until he had become "a true king" over one united Norway. Gyda then became one of his many wives. King Harold had at least a dozen wives, and many children.]

      Ragnvald Mørejarl var den kjæreste venn kong Harald hadde, og kongen satte han svært høgt. Ragnvald jarl var gift med Hild, datter til Rolv Nevja (Nese); deira sønner var Rolv og Tore. Ragnvald jarl hadde nokre frillesønner også; ein heit Hallad; ein annen Einar og ein tredje Rollaug; dei var voksne då dei ektefødte brødrene deires var barn endå.


      Earl Ragnvald was married to Hild, daughter of Rolv Nevja (Nose) [NES? Nes in Hjørundfjord? Ref also: "The Naze" = Lindesnes, a head-land or point.] Their sons were Rolv and Tore. Earl Ragnvald had some "frille" [frills? fringe?] sons also; one named Hallad; another Einar and a third Rollaug [="Rollo"?] ...

      Rolv var ein stor viking; han var så svær av vekst at ingen hest kunne bære han, derfor gjekk han til fots overalt. Han vart kalt «Gange-Rolv.» - - (Ein sommer hogg han strandhogg i Viken.) - - Kong Harald var i Viken; han vart alvorleg sint - - (Kongen gjorde Rolv fredlaus i Noreg.) - -


      Rolv was a great Viking; physically, he was so large that no horse could carry him, therefore he mostly walked everywhere. He was called "Walking-Ralph." - - One summer he plundered Viken in Norway. [King Harold was a guest at Viken at the time.] King Harold became extremely angered and banished Rolv from Norway.

      Gange-Rolv seilte - - til Valland (Frankrike) og herjet der, og vant seg eit stort jarlerike, som han bygde for ein stor del med nordmenn, og det heitar Normandi siden. Jarlene i Normandi er kommet av Rolvs ætt. Sønn til Gange-Rolv var Viljalm, far til Rikard, far til Rikard den andre, far til Robert Landspade, far til Viljalm Bastard, Englands konge. Frå han er alle Englandskongene ættet siden.


      "Ralph Walker" sailed to "Valland" (France) and won for himself [and his "North-men" or "Norsemen" followers & colonists] a very large earldom, which has ever since been called Normandy. The Earls of Normandy are of Rolv's lineage. Gange-Rolv's son was William, father of Richard, father of Richard the second [Duke of Normandy, d.1026]   father of Robert Landspade [also called "Robert the Devil" d.22 July 1035]   father of William the Bastard [as he was the illegitimate son of a tanner's daughter, but later he was known as "William the Conquerer", d.Sept. 1087] England's king. Of him, are all of England's royal kings descended.


Sjå "slektsband" tavle (under)..

See "Family Ties" table (below)..

      Då Kong Harald var førti år gammel, var mange av sønnene hans voksne karer, dei var tidleg modne alle sammen. Då gjekk det etter kvart slik at dei vart misnøgde med at kongen ikkje gav dem noko rike, men satte ein jarl i kvart fylke; dei meinte jarlene var av mindre ætt dei, enn dei sjølv var.


      When King Harold was 40 years old, many of his sons were grown men. They all matured early.

      Ein vår la dei av sted, Halvdan Hålegg (langbein) og Gudrød Ljome, med ein stor flokk og kom uventet over Ragnvald Mørejarl, kringsatte huset for han og brente han inne med 60 mann. Så tok Halvdan tre langship, fant mannskap til dem og seilte vest over havet. Gudrød satte seg fast i dei landene som Ragnvald jarl hadde hatt før.

      Kong Harald satte Tore, sønn til Ragnvald jarl over Møre, og giftet han med Ålov, datteren sin, ho som vart kalt Årbot. Tore jarl Teiande (den tause) fikk då same riket som hans far Ragnvald hadde hatt.


      King Harold declared Tore (The Quiet One) son of the slain Ragnvald, Earl of Møre, to be the new Earl over Møre, and wed him to Princess Ålov (called Årbot) his daughter.

VILJALM eroberen - - William the Conquerer
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
      William The Conquerer, Ruler of England, Duke of Normandy, is descended from the Norman-Viking Rollo = "Gange-Rolv" of Sunnmøre. A statue of Gange-Rolv is erected in Aalesund Park.


      A FRENCH CONNECTION?... William's father was "Robert the Devil," Duke of Normandy. He was a man widely-known for his extreme violent temper. William's mother was Arletta, a tanner's daughter.


      Robert was the younger son of Duke Richard II and he ruled Normandy after his older brother died. (He was accused of poisoning him!) Robert sheltered the exiled English princes, Edward (who became king Edward the Confessor) and his brother Alfred, and tried to help restore them to their inheritance. Robert died while returning from a pilgrimage to Jeruselem. He was the subject of many stories, poems and even an opera, "Robert le Diable."


      According to legend, even before he reached 20, Robert the Devil had amazing strength which he only used for crime and brutality. His wicked impulses were finally explained to him by his mother: She had called to the Devil, and had asked the Devil for another son. So, he was "The Devil's Answer" to her prayers. Robert beseeched the Pope for help for his soul to be rescued. The Pope directed him to a holy hermit.


      The hermit made Robert vow to never ever speak again, to feign madness and provoke ill-treatment but without retaliating, and to share food from the mouth of a dog. He became "Court Fool" to the Emperor in Rome. Over the course of 3 years, 3 heavenly messengers told him to protect the Holy City from the invading Arab Saracens. This he did in the guise of "The Unknown Knight" thrice saving Rome. The emperor's dumb daughter miraculously recovered her speech, and revealed that "The Unknown Knight" was really the poor abused Court Fool. Robert refused the hand of the beautiful princess, and instead returned to the holy hermit. There, he too ended his days as a hermit.


      THE COURTSHIP OF MATILDE of FLANDERS... When William, son of "Robert the Devil," first came courting his fair-haired relative, Matilde, in her castle, she laughingly rejected him saying, "Should I, a noblewoman, marry a bastard boy? Never!" At which point he threw her across the room, and the next room, and most other rooms and stairways before he left. After that, she had a respectful change of heart, and she persued him instead.


      William married (his first cousin?) Matilde of Flanders, daughter of Baldwin V of Flanders. She was descended from "Alfred the Great," the king of England, who in 878 had.. "saved England and Western Europe from the danger of becoming a heathen Scandinavian power base."


      William was related to King Edward the Confessor of England. His Aunt Emma was the Queen of England, and Edward's mother.. When his cousin Edward died, William claimed the throne of England as his own. There were other claimants too.

      Kong Olav hadde vært på hærferd vest i Valland to somrer og en vinter. Da hadde det gått tretten år siden kong Olav Trygveson falt.


      King Olav had been on military maneuvers [west in?] in [West?] France for 2 summers and one winter. It had then been 13 years since King Olav, son of Trygve, had fallen.

      Dengang var det to jarler i Valland, Viljalm og Robert, far deres var Rikard Rudajarl (Ruda = Rouen), de rådde over Normandi. Søster deres var dronning Emma, som hadde vært gift med kong Adalråd av England, og deres sønner var Edmund og Edvard den gode, Edvig og Edgar.


      At that time, there were 2 Earls in France, William and Robert, Their father [?] was Richard, Duke of Rouen. They ruled over Normandy. Their [?] sister was Queen Emma, who had been married to King Aethelred of England, and their sons were Edmund, Edward the Good [the Confessor] Edvig, and Edgar.

      Rikard Rudajarl var sønn til Rikard, sønn til Viljalm Langspyd, han var sønn til Gange-Rolv jarl som vant Normandi, og han var sønn av Ragnvald den mektige, Mørejarlen, som før skrevet.


      Richard, Duke of Rouen was the son of Richard, son of William Long-Spear. He was the son of Gange-Rolv [Ralph Walker] the Earl that conquered Nomandy, and he was the son of Ragnvald the Great, Earl of Møre, as written before.

      Fra Gange-Rolv er Rudajarlens ætt kommet, og regnet seg som skyldfolk til Norges høvdinger lenge etterpå og tenkte på dem som frender i lange tider, de var alltid beste venner med nordmennene; alle nordmenn som vilde kunne få fredland der hos dem.


      Gange-Rolv is the origin of the Dukes of Rouen's lineage, and they have reckoned themselves as [skyldfolk = beholden?] kin to Norway's chieftains ever since; and thought of them as relatives for a long time. They were always the best of friends with Norsemen. All Norsemen that wished to could get "peace-land" [refugee?] there with them.

      Om høsten kom kong Olav til Normandi og ble der i Signa (Seinen) om vinteren, og hadde fredland der.


      In the fall, Norway's King Olav came to Normandy and stayed there on the Seine during the winter, og had "peace-land" [refuge?] there.
