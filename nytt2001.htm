Chicago Firm Begins Building Animal Feed Supplement Plant in Eddyville, Iowa.

Knight Ridder/Tribune Business News; 2/12/2001

By Ed Trandahl, Omaha World-Herald, Neb.

Feb. 12--EDDYVILLE, Iowa--A new plant under construction here will manufacture a low-cost animal feed supplement that will also be more environmentally friendly, an executive of Ajinomoto Heartland Inc. said Friday.

The $39 million plant, a wing of another animal feed supplement plant built by the company in the mid-1980s, will manufacture 10,000 metric tons of feed-grade threonine, an amino acid used to stimulate animal growth, said Akira Suzuki, vice president at the company's Chicago office...

  •   26. mai 2001 -

Trandal turisme: ..."DDE" konserten i Aalesund.....    Store og små tilhengere fikk DDE navnetrekk på kort, plakater, t-skjorter og CD-er...      På ettermiddagen hadde vår gode venn Gunnar Nakken ordnet en liten utflukt for oss. Med chartret båt gikk turen inn Hjørundsfjorden til Christian Gård, (Trandal) et selskapslokale eller kro om du vil, litt utenom det vanlige. Her ble vi servert intet mindre enn grillet kjøtt av villsvin, kenguru og reinsdyr! Nydelig....      Vert på Christian gård, Frank Storeide, serverte gårdshistorien, samt sin nyeste nidvise....      I naborommet holdt et 50-årslag til, og ingen ringere enn Jens Petter Ekornes ("møbelkongen") dukket opp og bad om litt underholdning til jubilanten. Det ordnet seg...

     Trandal tourism: ...The "DDE" concert in Aalesund...   Big and small fans got DDE autographs on cards, posters, tee shirts and CDs...      In the afternoon our good friend Gunnar Nakken had arranged a litle trip for us. By chartered boat we toured Hjørundfjord to Christian Gård, (Trandal) a restaurant a bit out of the ordinary. Here we were served nothing less than grilled wild boar, kangaroo, and reindeer! Tasty....      The host of Christian Gaard, Frank Storeide, told the history of the farm, and sang his latest tunes...      In the neighboring room was a 50-year's celebration, and none other than Jens Petter Ekornes ("the furniture king") popped in and asked us for a bit of entertainment for the celebrant. No problem...
