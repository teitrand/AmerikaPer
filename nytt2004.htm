  •   25. mai 2004 -

Nyhendingsmedia gledeleg utroper Trandal lisenssaken "ein pikant farse" ...ein bitande politisk komedie.. mykje fram og tilbake borte på Ørsta formannskapsmøtet, med politikkarer som slåst over kvar ord og paragraf, ..Politiske utvalgte kontroll- og tilsynskomite'er er og med i leiken...

     The newsmedia gleefully calls the Trandal licensing case "a piquant farce" ...a stinging political comedy.. and describes ridiculous proceedings over at the Ørsta Mayor's Council meeting, with politicians fighting over each word & paragraph, .. Politically appointed inspection commitees are also joining the fun....

Kommentar - Commentary:
     But, meanwhile, ...back on isolated Trandal, it's not quite as funny. One family wonders how they'll make a living there. The rest of Trandal wonders how the final outcome will effect them. Other Trandalers around the world follow the case via the Internet.

     After her husband's liquor license was revoked, in order to continue the family's well-known tourist pub, the wife at "Christian Gaard" applied for licenses in her own right, under her own name. Apparently, the correct sequence of forms, papers, etc., were not filed the right way. Opposition politicians were upset that the Mayor had stepped in to give "Christian Gaard" a temporary permit until today's meeting, rather than shut down the family business during the busy tourist season. They were also upset that the Mayor opened his meeting to the press. It was supposed to be held secretly, behind closed doors.

     The outcome was that "Christian Gaard" will stay in business, at least for the time being. The question now is: What will happen in other future meetings? Apparently, this Trandal case may have become a victim of "party politics" over in Ørsta's town hall. Early in June the general "Control Commitee" meets. They may reverse today's positive action because the sequence of the submission of paperwork was not exactly correct. Then comes the "Food Control Commitee" which will hold their own "Christian Gaard" meetings.
